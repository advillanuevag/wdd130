History

Early civilizations relied on rivers for irrigation, commerce, and travel. Ancient Egyptians navigated the Nile using primitive rafts made of papyrus reeds, while indigenous peoples of the Americas crafted sturdy canoes from wood to traverse the mighty waterways of the continent.

Rafting, also known as whitewater rafting, traces its roots back to ancient times when rivers were essential routes for trade, exploration, and transportation. What began as a necessity evolved into a thrilling recreational activity enjoyed by adventurers worldwide.
